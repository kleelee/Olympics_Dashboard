About the Olympic Games

From 776 BC to 393 AD, the Ancient Olympic Games were held in Greece. In 1896, the first modern Olympic Games were held in Athens.




The Modern Olympic Games

The Ancient Olympics began in the Summer of 776 BC, and were held in Greece every four years until 393 AD. After this, it took 1503 years before the Olympics returned to modern day.

The Olympics creates an international playing field, with 206 National Olympic Committees(NOCs) competing in the Games. The first International Olympic Committee meeting was held in June 1984. The committee rightfully chose Greece as the site of the inaugural modern Olympics.

From 1924-1992, the Winter and the Summer Olympics took place in the same year. Now, they are on separate cycles and alternate every two years. Then next Olympic Games will be held in the Summer of 2020 in Tokyo, Japan.
